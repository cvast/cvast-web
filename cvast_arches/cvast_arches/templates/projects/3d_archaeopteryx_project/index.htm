Global

National Geographic Explorer Dr. Ryan Carney is an Assistant Professor of Digital Science in the Department of Integrative Biology and CVAST. His interdisciplinary work primarily focuses on flying dinosaurs and fighting diseases. This includes using new technologies to reconstruct the skeletons and original colors of fossil creatures, as well as predicting outbreaks of mosquito-borne viruses. (www.ryancarney.com)

Title

The 3D Archaeopteryx Project uses x-ray imaging, photogrammetry, and computer animation to reconstruct the best-preserved specimens of the icon of evolution, Archaeopteryx (ar-kee-OP-ter-icks). This so-called “missing link” between dinosaurs and birds holds the key to solving the mystery of how flight evolved. But could Archaeopteryx actually fly? And is it truly the first known bird?
